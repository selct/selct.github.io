Posted on November 19, 2016 at 8:23 PM

SELCT was well-represented at the recent Electronics New Zealand Conference (ENZCon) 2016. PhD students Blake Johnston and Jason Long gave talks based upon their peer-reviewed papers during the November 17th and 18th conference held at Victoria University of Wellington

ENZCon is a New Zealand-wide conference that focuses on bringing together academics, students, technicians, and those in industry. In its 23rd year, ENZcon has been held at various New Zealand universities during its previous sessions. This year, SELCT researcher Jim Murphy coordinated the conference and edited the conference proceedings.

Blake Johnston and co-author (and SELCT alumnus) Bridget Johnson presented a talk on their paper “A New Framework for the Interactive Control of Mechatronic Instruments”. The paper (co-authored with SELCT faculty member Ajay Kapur) focuses on the development of apps and interfaces that may be used to control high degree-of-freedom mechatronic instruments and installations.

Jason Long presented “Improving the Accuracy of Musical Robots with Programmable Logic Circuits” (co-authored with SELCT faculty members Jim Murphy and Dale Carnegie). This paper focused on the develoment of a highly accurate pulse timer, allowing mechatronic percussion systems to be precisely controlled. This precise control allows for previously-unattainable levels of dynamic control on percussion-based musical robots. Jason accompanied his talk with a demonstration of his feedback-equipped robotic glockenspiel (shown above).

ENZCon 2016 was a great chance for SELCT students and faculty to engage in discussions with engineers, gaining feedback about the engineering-oriented aspects of their work. SELCT aims to have an equally large presence at ENZCon 2017, slated to be held at the University of Canterbury.
