FACILITIES, LABS, HARDWARE, SOFTWARE

SELCT student researchers have access to a number of cutting-edge music and engineering facilities. These facilities include rapid prototyping equipment, music production studios, and lab workshops. Additionally, student researchers have access to a wide variety of software and hardware useful in the production of sonic artworks and engineering projects. Further, as members of a large research university, SELCT researchers may gain access to the wider production facilities of faculties school-wide.

Student researchers are provided desk-space in well-equipped engineering labs, with access to state-of-the-art measurement tools and computers equipped with industry-leading CAD software. Alongside these engineering studios, a number of music production suites are on-site, complete with professional-grade music hardware and software.

NZSM SONIC ARTS LAB

New Zealand School of Music

The New Zealand School of Music's Sonic Arts Lab is a space intended for the development of music hardware and software. Equipped with an electronics workshop and workbenches, the lab may also be used for small concerts, presentations, and lectures.

POSTGRADUATE LABS

VUW School of Engineering and Computer Science

SELCT postgraduates (those working toward PhD and Masters degrees) are provided desks in engineering labspaces. These labs are complete with computers (equipped with a variety of software including SolidWorks, MATLAB, and Altium) and measurement tools, in addition to well-equipped electronics workbenches.

RAPID PROTOTYPING LABS

VUW School of Engineering and Computer Science

SELCT researchers have access to a number of high-performance rapid-prototyping tools. These tools include a 70-Watt laser cutter, a wide variety of FDM-style 3D printers, and high-precision PCB milling CNC routers. Additionally, students may approach VUW's on-site machine shop for CNC lathe and mill-based work.

LILBURN STUDIOS

New Zealand School of Music

New Zealand School of Music's Lilburn Studios are three studio spaces equipped with professional-grade audio production equipment. In addition to an isolation booth, an assortment of microphones and outboard gear, the studios are equipped with a number of vintage modular synthesizers.

MECHANICAL WORKSHOP

VUW School of Engineering and Computer Science

The VUW School of Engineering and Computer Science provides students with access to a mechanical workshop. This shop is equipped with a number of power tools, fasteners, and raw materials.

ADAM CONCERT ROOM

New Zealand School of Music

SELCT researchers may present their artworks at the New Zealand School of Music's Adam Concert Room. This concert space is equipped with a number of instruments, lighting facilities, and recording options.

MECHATRONIC INSTRUMENTS

New Zealand School of Music, VUW School of Engineering and Computer Science

SELCT researchers have developed one of the world's leading collections of new mechatronic instruments. Researchers may use these instruments for compositional and research projects.
