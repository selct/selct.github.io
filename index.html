ABOUT

SELCT (Sonic Engineering Lab for Creative Technology) is a research group located at Victoria University of Wellington in New Zealand. SELCT’s focuses are on mechatronic music, interface design, music information retrieval and pedagogy, and sound art for performance and installation contexts. As an interdisciplinary research group, many projects combine music and engineering practices.

RESEARCH & ARTWORKS

A core focus of SELCT is on the creation of compelling artworks: this dedication to artistic excellence manifests itself in all aspects of our work, ranging from our installations and performances through to our academic outputs. Our researchers focus on kinetic sound sculpture, mechatronic instruments, new musical interfaces, and music information retrieval.

GET INVOLVED

SELCT is actively recruiting those interested in joining and contributing to our research environment! At the doctoral level, PhD and DMA programmes are available. For those interested in pursuing a masters degree, you may pursue a masters in Engineering or in Music. At the undergraduate level, SELCT researchers offer a number of innovative courses in our areas of study.

victoria university of wellington

SELCT involves researchers from Victoria University of Wellington’s Te Kōkī New Zealand School of Music and School of Engineering and Computer Science. One of New Zealand’s leading universities, Victoria University of Wellington is located in central Wellington at the southern end of New Zealand’s North Island.

As an active research university in both the sciences and humanities, Victoria University of Wellington offers students the change to pursue interdisciplinary studies (such as those emphasised by SELCT) at the postgraduate and undergraduate levels.
